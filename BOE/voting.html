Voter Registration

To register to vote you must:
  • Be a United States citizen
  • Be 18 years old by December 31st of the year in which you file your registration form (note - you must be 18 years old by the date of the general, primary, or other election in which you want to vote)
  • Be a resident of St. Lawrence County for at least 30 days prior to the election
  • NOT be in prison or on parole for a felony conviction (unless parolee pardoned or restored rights of citizenship)
  • NOT be adjudged mentally incompetent by a court
  • NOT be claiming the right to vote elsewhere

Accesible Registration Form can be found here.

Standard Registration Form can be found here.

Note: You may pre-register at 16 or 17 but cannot vote until you are 18 years old.

*If you want to vote in an election, you must mail or deliver your completed registration form to the Board of Elections NO LATER THAN 25 DAYS BEFORE the election in which you want to vote. Your eligibility to vote will be based on the date you file this form, and the county board will notify you of such*

Absentee Voting

To Vote by Absentee Ballot, one must be one of the following:
  • Absent from St. Lawrence County on election day
  • Unable to appear at the polls due to illness or disability
  • Unable to appear at the polls due to duties related to primary care of one or more individuals who are ill or physically disabled
  • A patient in a Veteransâ€™ Administration Hospital
  • Detained in jail awaiting Grand Jury action or confined in prison after conviction for an offense other than a felony

Regular Absentee Ballot can be found here.

To Vote by Permanently Disabled Absentee Ballot, one must have one of the following:
  • Permanent illness
  • Physical disability

Permanently Disabled Absentee Ballot can be found here.

To Vote by Military or Federal Absentee Ballot, one must be one of the following:
  • Activated National Guard member on State orders
  • Actively in the Military, eligible spouse, or dependent

Military or Federal Absentee Ballot can be found here.

To Vote by Special Federal Absentee Ballot, one must be one of the following:
  • A United States Citizen who is living abroad whose last address was in St. Lawrence County
  • A United States Citizen who has never lived in the U.S.

Special Federal Absentee Ballot can be found here.

*Deadlines for Absentee Ballots vary and are stated on each form*

Early Voting

During the two weeks leading up to each special, primary or general election (see Upcoming Elections)

Where:

Canton Board of Elections Office (Second Floor of Human Services Building, 80 State Highway 310)
